Leadership

Co-President, Union College Robotics Crew
Schenectady, New York

  • Designed, constructed, and programed autonomous robots for maze-solving competitions, including Brown Robotics Competition and IEEE Regional Micromouse Competition.
  • Explained and assisted new members with hardware/software debugging, wiring and soldering, and understanding various electrical components.
  • Demonstrated maze-solving algorithms (i.e. Floodfill and Breath-first Search) during college's annual Steinmetz Symposiums.
  • Promoted the Robotics Crew and recruited members during annual Club Expositions and class presentations.

Co-Leader, The Pre-Collegiate Program of Yangon
Yangon, Burma

  • Participated as a member of the planning committee and led eighteen Stanford Volunteers in Asia (VIA) members, who had volunteered in Asia 30 to 40 years ago.
  • Elected as co-leader and led a group of students from Thailand, Taiwan, and Japan from Learning Across Borders (LAB).
  • Actively engaged as a member of the Touchstones Discussion Council. Managed logistics which included researching readings for the discussions, recruiting guest speakers for presentations, and arranging refreshments for discussion participants.

Assistant Coach, Lumbini Academy
Yangon, Burma

  • Instructed and supported students, some whom are autistic, in sports including soccer, basketball, kickball, ultimate Frisbee, and dodge ball.
  • Cooperated with the Board of Directors to initiate the first International Day ceremony and fun fair at the Lumbini Academy.

Assistant Archaeologist, The Henry Luce Foundation
Bagan, Burma

  • Led the 2013-14 Luce Scholars on an archaeological tour in the ancient city of Bagan, and organized a discussion about a Bagan research paper written during PCP, on Buddha statues and temples from the 11th to 13th century.

President, US Embassy American Center Student Council
Yangon, Burma

  • Worked hand-in-hand with 60 volunteers and recruited new volunteers to promote and defend civic and democratic responsibilities in a dictatorship.
  • Planned and executed campus wide events, which ranged from Christmas, Halloween, Thingyan (Burmese Water Festival) ceremonies to Charity events, debate series, writing contests, and leadership workshops.
  • Coordinated the first campus-wide certificate giving ceremony for students.
  • Served as liaison between students and officials from the U.S. Embassy, which included Derek Mitchell (US Ambassador to Burma), Ann Stock (US Assistant Secretary of State for Educational and Cultural Affairs), and Natalie I. Bonjoc (Assistant Public Affairs Officer).
  • Raised a total of $3000 and donated the proceeds to earthquake victims and war refugees in Northern Burma.

Chair, US Embassy American Center Student Affairs Committee
Yangon, Burma

  • Created and compiled Practice TOEFL and SAT I, II tests for the students and library patrons of the American Center.
  • Hosted TOEFL and SAT pre-tests twice-weekly (Monday and Saturday) and assisted students as a peer educator on strategies to improve writing and English skills.

Country Representative, Southeast Asia Youth Leadership Program
DeKalb, IL

  • Planted trees, painted bird houses, and made face paintings with home stay families.
  • Selected as the Master of Ceremonies for the Farewell Dinner to deliver the opening speech and oversee stage directions.
